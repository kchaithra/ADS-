India, officially the Republic of India, is a country in South Asia. It is the seventh-largest country by land area, the second-most populous country, and the most populous democracy in the world. Bordered by the Indian Ocean on the south, the Arabian Sea on the southwest, and the Bay of Bengal on the southeast, it shares land borders with Pakistan to the west; China, Nepal, and Bhutan to the north; and Bangladesh and Myanmar to the east. In the Indian Ocean, India is in the vicinity of Sri Lanka and the Maldives; its Andaman and Nicobar Islands share a maritime border with Thailand, Myanmar, and Indonesia. Home to the ancient Indus Valley Civilization and a region of historic trade routes and vast empires, the Indian subcontinent was identified with its commercial and cultural wealth for much of its long history. Famous Indian personalities include Mahatma Gandhi, Jawaharlal Nehru, Indira Gandhi, and Narendra Modi, who have played significant roles in shaping the nation's history and politics. India's diverse culture, languages, religions, and traditions are some of the distinctive features that contribute to its richness and complexity. The country's economy is one of the fastest-growing major economies in the world, with sectors such as information technology, telecommunications, and pharmaceuticals playing crucial roles in its development.
